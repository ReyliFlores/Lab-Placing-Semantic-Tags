Silicon Chip Implant Marvel

right! Chandan Sen and his team at the Indiana University School of Medicine have developed a groundbreaking nanochip device called tissue nanotransfection. This device can reprogram skin cells in the body to become new blood vessels and nerve cells, offering potential treatments for various health issues, including repairing brain damage caused by stroke and preventing nerve damage from diabetes

Legal Implications, IU School Of Medicine

The legal implications of tissue nanotransfection (TNT) technology are still evolving.The use of gene editing and tissue reprogramming raises ethical questions, such as the potential for unintended genetic changes and the long-term effects on patients.

Other Areas of Research

Nanotechnology is a rapidly evolving field with research spanning various areas. Developing targeted drug delivery systems, advanced diagnostics, and regenerative medicine techniques. Using nanotechnology for gene editing, tissue engineering, and creating wearable devices for health monitoring
